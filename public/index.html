Chess Experiment

You have been invited to take part in a cognitive study that is looking at the process of finding a chess move. Whether or not you take part is your choice. If you do not want to take part, you do not have to give a reason. If you do want to take part now but change your mind later, you can pull out of the study at any time.

Participant’s Rights

You are under no obligation to accept this invitation. If you decide to participate, you have the right to:

  • decline to answer any particular question
  • withdraw from the study (up to 6 weeks from the date of your participation)
  • ask any questions about the study at any time during participation
  • provide information on the understanding that your name will not be used
  • be given access to a summary of the project findings when it is concluded
WHAT IS THE PURPOSE OF THE STUDY?

The purpose of this study is to examine all the possible moves that can be made in a chess position. This study aims to build on cognitive skill studies that examine the thought process for finding a chess move.

WHAT WILL MY PARTICIPATION IN THE STUDY INVOLVE?

You have been chosen to participate because you meet the criteria of the study. The criteria include:

  • chess players with a Fide rating above 2000 (experts)
  • amateur chess players
  • to be able to read and type in English

The study will involve you selecting your best move from 12 given chess positions in three blocks of four chess positions using your computer mouse. You will have a maximum of TO FILL IN to make your move in each position. Please do not google or use devices to help you to select your move as we are interested in all the possible moves that players might make in the position shown. Firstly, you will see a practice chess position. You can practice moving chess pieces using your computer mouse. You can practice as many times as you like. After you have practiced enough to be familiar with the chess set, you can then select the tab to take you to Block One.

Allow at least TO FILL IN to complete the tasks. You will be presented with the words “Block One” and you push the start button to begin. Once you push start, the first chess position with the same interface as the practice chess position will appear. Take note of the clock counting down as this is the time you have in which to make your move. The position is finished as soon as you have placed a chess piece on an empty square or captured a piece with your computer mouse. Then the next position will appear. Once you have made your last move in the final fourth position by placing a chess piece on an empty square or capturing a piece, you will then be asked to complete a brief questionnaire. Once you have pushed submit on your questionnaire, you will see the words “Block two” with a start button appear. Once again after pushing start, the first chess position with a clock counting down will appear, and the position will be completed once you made your move by releasing the chess piece with your computer mouse on an empty square or by capturing a piece. Then the next position will appear. Once again after completing the final fourth position in Block Two, you will be asked to complete a brief questionnaire. After completing this, Block Three will appear with a start button. Once you pushed start, the first chess position with a clock counting down will appear, and the position will be completed once you made your move by releasing the chess piece with your computer mouse on an empty square or by capturing a piece. The next position will appear after this. Once again after placing a chess piece on an empty square or capturing a piece to complete the final fourth position in Block Three, you will be asked to complete a brief questionnaire. Once this is submitted, you have successfully completed the study.

WHAT ARE THE POSSIBLE BENEFITS AND RISKS OF THIS STUDY?

Direct benefits of participating in this study include an increased awareness and knowledge of the processes involved in research by actively participating in it and a satisfaction in knowing that you are contributing to cognitive skill studies and chess. Additionally, there is also a monetary payment for involvement in this study.

Foreseeable risks, adverse-effects, and discomforts that you may encounter by taking part in this study are minimal.

WHAT ARE MY RIGHTS?

Participating in this study is completely voluntary, and you are free to decline to participate or to withdraw from the research at any practicable time without experiencing any disadvantage.

You, the participant, has a right to access information about you collected as part of this study.

It is important to us that we maintain your privacy throughout this study. Your name and contact information will be held electronically and stored on the principal investigator’s computer only for data recording purposes. Each participant in the study will be allocated a number. Staff involved in analysis will have access to participants’ numbers only. All data from test sessions will be recorded against your participant ID number and your name will never be used in any report, correspondence or publication.

Your involvement in this study is confidential.

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER THE STUDY OR IF I CHANGE MY MIND?

You can withdraw from the study at any time and will be compensated accordingly for your time. Further, you are welcome to discuss any concerns you have with the research team at any time, and you have free access to your data. If you pull out of the study, all data that was related to you will be destroyed.

Block 1

You will have a maximum of TO FILL IN to make a move in each of the four positions presented, but you may find that you can complete the move in less time. A timer will appear on the screen, displaying the time counting down.

TO FILL IN

Your games are important but not necessarily the final round when you are playing for big money.

You have decided that you can allow TO FILL IN for making a move in each of the four positions in order to still have sufficient time on your clock for the remainder of the games.

Do not do more or less analysis than you would in the above situation, i.e., don’t “show off” for the exercise. This is not a “play and win” problem. It is supposed to be an interesting position from a real game and so to look for a “winning” – as opposed to a “best” – move may be a futile effort.

Practice Position

The next position is a practice position for you to practice making a move. Click on the piece you want to move. Once you release the piece on a free square or capture another piece, the move is made. You can then practice again by selecting the practice tab or you can start the first chess position if you need no further practice. Note that once you release the piece on a free square or capture another piece, the move is final, and you move on to the next position.
